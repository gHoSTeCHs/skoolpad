Authoring handoff

The artefact handed to Content Studio.

When an editor declares a canonical topic ready, Curriculum Studio compiles a single JSON document — the topic brief — and ships it to Content Studio. The brief is the contract: it carries the canonical definition, every source excerpt that supports it, the past-question calibration, the visualization brief, and the class-assignment timeline. Content Studio authors against the brief verbatim.

Topic § 1.2 · Physics · Senior Secondary I · Term 1

Position, distance, displacement

Class assignment confirmed across NERDC, WAEC, JAMB and the platform-shared scheme. Past-question calibration drawn from 8 indexed papers, 2008–2022.

Position locates a body in space relative to a reference point. Distance is the total length of the path traversed by the body — a scalar quantity, measured in metres. Displacement is the shortest straight-line interval between the initial and final positions — a vector quantity carrying both magnitude and direction. Distance and displacement coincide only when motion proceeds in a single straight direction without reversal; in every other case displacement is strictly less than distance.

Source manifest

WAEC syllabus § 1 · topic 1.2 · 2026 ed.
Distinction between distance and displacement; concepts of speed, velocity, and acceleration. Candidates should be able to: distinguish between distance and displacement; identify motion in a straight line; calculate distance and displacement when given coordinates.
JAMB syllabus § 1.2 · 2025/26 ed.
Position, distance and displacement: scalar and vector representations. Candidates are expected to define each quantity, distinguish their units of measurement, and compute the resultant displacement of two perpendicular components.
NERDC scheme of work SS1 · T1 · theme 1 · topic § 4
The student shall be led to differentiate between distance and displacement using the school compound, the football field, or the journey from home to school. Activities to include: walking distances measured against straight-line displacement; representation of displacement as a directed line segment; introduction of vector notation.
Platform-shared scheme SS1 · T1 · week 3
Build on intuition from Junior Secondary motion topics. Suggested visualization: animated journey from school gate, through the playground, to the assembly point, with displacement vector overlaid. Recommended class test: short calculation of two-leg perpendicular journey.

Past-question calibration

Across 8 indexed papers (2008–2022) one calculation pattern dominates: the two-leg perpendicular displacement. Any worked example authored for this topic should exercise that pattern.

Anchor question · WAEC Nov 2011 · Paper 2 · Q 1
1.A car travels 30 metres due east, then 40 metres due north. Calculate, correct to the nearest whole number, the magnitude of its displacement and the angle the displacement makes with the easterly direction.
Recurrence pattern across calibration corpus: two-leg perpendicular 6/8 papers · three-leg open polygon 1/8 · circular path 1/8. Recommended difficulty band: standard. Estimated time-to-solve: 90s.

Why it earns its place

What Curriculum Studio unlocks.

🎓

WAEC-grade content

Briefs ship with anchor past questions. Content Studio's worked examples now match the actual style and difficulty of the exam. Student opens a block, recognises the question type, builds calibrated confidence.

🧮

JAMB-aware authoring

Topics carry JAMB linkage flags. SS3 content can be authored with explicit awareness of which questions appear on UTME, calibrated to the 30-second-per-MCQ format JAMB rewards.

⚡

Faster authoring loops

Content Studio stops rediscovering topic structure on every prompt. Briefs cut model context by ~60% and let the model focus on prose — not on guessing what to write about.

🪜

Vertical curriculum awareness

The class-assignment timeline knows when SS3's Mechanical energy is a revisit of SS1's Work, energy, power. Spaced repetition can schedule reviews across years, not just within them.

Architecturally, Curriculum Studio sits at /admin/curriculum/*, a sibling module to Content Studio — not nested inside it. Curriculum data is a foundational concern that belongs at the same level as disciplines, education_levels, and curriculum_subjects. Burying it inside an authoring tool would be the wrong abstraction.
